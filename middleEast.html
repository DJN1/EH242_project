Christian Creation Myth

Genesis (Priestly Source)

On the first day God created heaven and the earth. God then created light, saw that it was good, so he called light “day” and called darkness “night”.

On the second day he then created a firmament and called it “heaven”.

He then created dry land and called it earth. He created plants, and fruit-bearing trees on the third day.

On the fourth day God created the stars, the seasons, the sun and the moon.

God created water animals and the birds, and he let them reproduce and multiply.

On the sixth day God created cattle and other land animals. He then created man to rule over all other animals. He created male and female and told them to be “fruitful”. He instructs humans to eat the plants and animals to their desire.

God rested on the seventh day and blessed it to be the day of rest.

Information and summary based on
Thury, E. M. Introduction to Mythology. [Yuzu].
